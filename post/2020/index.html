2020

2021-01-04

Contents

  • Behind the scenes

Despite what most are saying about the year, twenty-twenty has been the best year of my life. Looking back, I’m proud of what I’ve done and who I’m choosing to become. It’s as if I’m finally at the age that I should be, as if I’ve just woken up from a deep slumber. Fair warning, this post is very much me, me, me, and lopsided on the positive end of the spectrum. I don’t intend to come off as egotistic or self centered, but this is my story, and if you’re interested, have a read.

My career has been a helluva ride. I’ve shifted into a role that is closer aligned on who I want to be, and it has given me insight into business like never before. I’m lucky to serve a strong team that has been constantly evolving, with a boss responsible for accelerating my growth and whom I deeply respect. The company is at a stage undergoing massive growth, with problems I’m so fortunate to own and be exposed to. I remember making the conscious decision to change my working environment with certain plans in mind, and I’m glad to say they have come to fruition.

This year I have read and listened more intensely than any other year of my life. I’ve gone through 18 audio books, and read through 4, partially 3, plus or minus here and there. I’ve experimented with journaling, todo lists, and have learned the stories of several strangers I’ve met online. It’s as if I’m finally beginning to discover universal truths I remember searching for when I was much younger. The lives of many are starting to have a common theme. Maybe I’m just seeing what I want to see, and I’m okay with using that to my advantage.

These past couple months I’ve also done a much better job at improving my general state well being. My living environment is much cleaner, the bed is made, clothes are folded, crumbs are swept, and I’ve got more places to sit. I’ve been cooking several different dishes, mostly vegan at that. Pro tip, if you are looking to lift yourself up, put the effort into cleaning your fridge and filling it with abundance. The first time I did, it really felt like self made luxury.

My key takeaways from the year are as follows:

  • Stimulus -> Choice -> Response
  • You are better off treating goals as symptoms rather than an objectives
  • There are many variable out of our control, focus only on increasing likelihood
  • Be opportunistic in serving your future self
  • Harbour effort into making the subconscious mind work for you
  • Put yourself first in a healthy manner, it is the selfless thing to do
  • Prioritize play
  • Love

I have many other learnings, but these are the first that come to mind at time of writing.

Given all this internal joy, I’m still acutely aware of my faults, and where I want to improve. I’m not certain who or what had the most influential impact in the improvement of mental well being. Perhaps my bar was so low, that I’m only now meeting the normal of most people. It could be the case I was in a deep rut, and my perception of normal is heaven. I carry deep appreciation for life, and I’m eager to pay it forward.

This year I’ll be striving to solidify a clear vision and mission, while making choices to live in alignment by what I define. I’m hoping to tap into the more creative side of things like content creation, dance and music. The future is exciting, and I welcome it with open arms. Wishing you a strong and fulfilling year.

Be curious and move forward.
